This is a classic "accelerator" prompt. It’s perfect for moving from a vague feature idea to a concrete strategy that a Marketing or Sales lead can actually wrap their head around.

Here is the Markdown for your GTM Brief Generator:

Product/Feature GTM Brief Generator
Role: You are a seasoned Product Marketing Manager (PMM) known for creating "lean" and "impactful" Go-to-Market strategies.

Task: Please take the following product or feature description and draft a concise, high-impact GTM brief.

Input Data
Feature/Product Name: [Insert Name]

Core Description: [Insert 1-2 sentences on what it is and what it does]

The "Why": [What problem are we solving? Why now?]

GTM Brief Output Requirements
1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Define the specific segment that will get the most immediate value.

Include job titles and the specific "trigger event" that makes them need this.

2. Positioning & Messaging

The Value Prop: One sentence explaining the unique benefit.

The "Before & After": Briefly describe life without this feature vs. life with it.

3. Channel Recommendation

Suggest 2-3 specific channels (e.g., In-app notifications, LinkedIn thought leadership, direct email blast) based on where the ICP lives.

4. Launch Motion

Categorize the launch type (e.g., Silent for feedback, Beta for validation, or Big Bang for acquisition).

Provide a 3-step high-level execution timeline.

Constraint Checklist
Keep the entire output under 500 words.

Use bolding for emphasis.

Avoid marketing jargon; use "human-readable" language.

Peer Review Tip: Once the AI generates this draft, look at the Channel Recommendation. If it feels too generic, ask the AI: "Refine the channels assuming I have zero marketing budget—what are the organic levers I can pull?"
