# Rules — Stakeholder Management

Confirmed patterns to apply by default. Demote to `HYPOTHESES.md` if later contradicted.

1. Assign exactly one Accountable (RACI) or Approver (DACI) per task or decision. A task or decision with zero or multiple final-say owners is the most common cause of stalled work or conflicting direction, and should be corrected as soon as noticed.

2. Reserve formal RACI/DACI matrices for genuinely cross-functional, ambiguous-ownership initiatives. Do not build one for routine tasks with obvious ownership; the overhead exceeds the coordination problem being solved.

3. Build a stakeholder power/interest map before a cross-functional initiative kicks off, and calibrate communication effort by quadrant (manage closely, keep satisfied, keep informed, monitor) rather than communicating uniformly to everyone.

4. Re-assess the power/interest map as the initiative progresses, not just once at the start. A stakeholder's position can shift materially, particularly from low interest to high interest once budget, scope, or headcount is affected.

5. When a product team finds itself needing to "manage closely" an unusually large number of high-power stakeholders on routine product decisions, treat that as a structural signal (closer to a feature factory than an empowered team), not just a communications problem to solve with more updates.
