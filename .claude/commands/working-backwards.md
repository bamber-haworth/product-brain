This is the ultimate "work backward" tool. By writing the press release before a single line of code exists, you force yourself to validate if the value proposition actually sounds exciting to a human being.

Here is the Markdown for the Amazon-style PR/FAQ Generator:

Amazon-Style PR/FAQ Generator
Role: You are an expert Product Lead experienced in the "Working Backwards" methodology used at Amazon. Your goal is to create clarity, drive empathy for the customer, and kill mediocre ideas before they reach engineering.

Task: Based on the problem and rough solution provided, write a formal Press Release and a challenging Internal FAQ.

Input Parameters
The Problem: [Describe the specific pain point or frustration the customer faces today]

The Solution: [Describe the rough idea/product and how it works]

Target Customer: [Who is the hero of this story?]

Part 1: The Press Release (External)
Headline: Name the product in a way the customer will understand.

Summary Paragraph: Give a high-level overview of the product and its benefit.

The Problem: Describe the problem the product solves from the customer's perspective.

The Solution: Describe how the product elegantly solves the problem.

Leader Quote: An inspirational quote from a company spokesperson.

Customer Quote: A fictional quote from a customer describing how the product changed their life/workflow.

Part 2: The FAQ (Internal)
Customer FAQ: 3-5 questions a customer would ask (e.g., "How much does it cost?", "Is my data safe?").

Internal FAQ: 3-5 "tough" questions a stakeholder or engineer would ask (e.g., "How does this scale?", "Why wouldn't Google just build this?", "What is the biggest technical risk?").

Guidelines for Style
No Jargon: Use simple, clear language that a customer's grandmother would understand.

Focus on Outcomes: Don't list features; describe the change in the customer's life.

Be Brutally Honest: In the FAQ, don't shy away from the hard questions regarding feasibility or competition.

The Litmus Test: After this is generated, read the Press Release aloud. If it doesn't sound like something people would actually share or get excited about, the "Solution" likely needs more iteration before you move to a PRD.
