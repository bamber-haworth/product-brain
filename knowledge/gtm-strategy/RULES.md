# Rules — GTM Strategy

Confirmed patterns to apply by default. Demote to `HYPOTHESES.md` if later contradicted.

1. Prefer needs-based or jobs-based segmentation over demographic/firmographic segmentation when the goal is predicting behaviour or informing positioning. Demographic variables are easy to obtain but weak predictors on their own; two customers identical on demographics can have entirely different underlying needs.

2. Check a candidate segment against all four criteria before treating it as actionable: measurable, substantial, accessible, differentiable. A segment that fails "differentiable" (responds no differently than other segments) is not a useful segment regardless of how cleanly it can be described.

3. State positioning relative to a named competitive alternative, not as a standalone description of product attributes. Positioning is a claim in the customer's mind relative to alternatives; without a stated alternative, a positioning claim cannot be evaluated.

4. Build market size estimates bottom-up (from evidenced unit economics: real or piloted ARPU, conversion rates, achievable customer count given actual channels) before citing top-down industry statistics, especially for an early-stage or unlaunched product. Use top-down figures to sanity-check the bottom-up number, not as the primary estimate.

5. Always state the boundary of a TAM figure explicitly (what is and is not included in the category) before comparing it to another source's TAM, since TAM figures with different implicit boundaries are not comparable.

6. Scope SAM and SOM to a specific target segment reached by a defined go-to-market motion, not to an undifferentiated total market. A SAM/SOM without a named target segment and channel is not yet a real estimate.
