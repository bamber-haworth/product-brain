# Rules — Product Improvement Methodology

Confirmed patterns to apply by default. Demote to `HYPOTHESES.md` if later contradicted.

---

**Segment users by how they use the product, not by who they are.** Demographic segments rarely reveal meaningfully different product needs. Behavioural segments derived from analytics (frequency, feature adoption, workflow completion) do. Default to behavioural segmentation as the primary organising principle.

**Do not generate improvement vectors before completing journey mapping.** Vectors without supporting friction evidence are solutions in search of a problem. Journey maps must come first; improvement directions are derived from the friction they surface.

**If the team cannot agree on the positioning statement, fix the positioning before doing improvement work.** Improvement directions are downstream of "for whom and why." Ambiguous positioning produces ambiguous improvement work, regardless of how rigorous the rest of the process is.
