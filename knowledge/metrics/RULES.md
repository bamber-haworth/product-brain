# Rules — Metrics

Confirmed patterns to apply by default. Demote to `HYPOTHESES.md` if later contradicted.

1. When defining metrics for a feature or product area, follow Goals-Signals-Metrics order: state the goal in plain language first, then identify observable signals of progress, then pick instrumentable metrics. Do not select a metric first because it is easy to measure and retrofit a goal to justify it.

2. Select only the HEART categories genuinely relevant to what is being measured. Do not mechanically fill in all five categories for every evaluation; an irrelevant category produces a weak or misleading metric.

3. Define "Activation" for a specific product deliberately, using a combination of usage-pattern analysis and correlation with long-term retention, rather than adopting a generic definition. A vague or borrowed activation definition undermines every funnel metric built on top of it.

4. When diagnosing a growth or adoption problem, plot conversion at each AARRR stage before deciding where to invest, and focus effort on the stage with the largest drop-off rather than spreading improvement work evenly across the whole funnel.

5. For multi-sided marketplaces, build separate AARRR funnels per side (supply and demand) rather than one combined funnel. A single blended funnel obscures which side is actually constraining growth.

6. Treat Happiness metrics (surveys, NPS) as a lagging, aggregate signal, not a basis for fast iteration. Use them to validate direction over a longer horizon, and rely on Engagement, Adoption, and Task success metrics for near-term decisions.
